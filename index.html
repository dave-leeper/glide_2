The Icosiheptagon and Icosiheptagram created from the 27 trigrams, along with lines connecting each point. Each line represents two hexagrams. Each of the 27 points represents one hexagram.

The three unicursal hexagrams of the trigrams (Above, In, and Below) can have their points placed on the points of an octadecagon. This octadecagon can further be dissected into 144 rhombi parallelograms, providing a home for the 144 hexagrams of the Zodiac I Ching. Each of the Zodiac I Ching trigrams can interact with the Glide 2.0 trigrams to form a hexagram. This will be examined in more detail in a future update.

The original version of the Glide Oracle was developed by Diana Slattery. It was modeled after the I Ching using symbols made from a deconstucted yin and yang symbol. Slatter refered to these symbols as a languange. It was claimed the symbols were recieved from aliens. The interpretation of the symbols' meaning seemed to be based off of a stream of consciousness impression made by each symbol.
Version 2.0 reinterpretes the symbols based on Daoism, which is where the yin and yang symbol is from. This reinterpretation consists of basing the 27 possible trigrams on existing Daoist principles. These 27 trigrams are then combined to form 729 hexagrams. The meanings of these hexagrams are built from the meanings of the two trigrams they contain. The top trigram represents external influences, while the bottom trigram represents internal influences.
